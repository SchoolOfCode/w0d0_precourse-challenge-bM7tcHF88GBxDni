STAR TREK
DEEP SPACE NINE

Breaking new ground, still unbeaten

Starting in 1993 and ending in 1999, Deep Space Nine is now regarded as one of the best science fiction shows ever created.

It's emotionally resonant and builds a political and complicated world. While there are plenty of episodic stories, Deep Space Nine is one of the first TV shows to do long form narrative arcs that are the norm today. It's an exceptional feat to remain one of the best serial TV shows 25 years later.

A large, diverse cast exploring human and alien societies and ideology

A plethora of 10 main cast members and up to 40 recurring roles lead to a world that feels real and independent. Characters have their own agency and interpersonal relationships, engrossing viewing as we find our characters in morally compromised and conflicting situations.

Deep Space Nine is a Cardassian built mining space station owned by the Bajorans and operated with the Federation. The first episode of Deep Space Nine starts after the end of an oppressive occupation of the planet Bajor by the Cardassians. The Bajoran Provisional Government requested Federation assistance and Commander Sisko is sent to the station in a joint venture with the Bajoran militia.


  • Benjamin Sisko, commanding Federation officer of the space station Deep Space Nine which orbits the planet of Bajor.

  • Kira Nerys, first officer of Deep Space Nine. Major Kira starts off on Deep Space Nine as a liaison on behalf of the Provincial Government of her home planet of Bajor.

  • Jadzia Dax, chief science officer. Jadzia lives in symbiosis with a long-lived creature, known as a symbiont, named Dax

  • Quark, a cunning bar owner.

  • Odo, chief of security on Deep Space Nine. Odo is a shape shifter who had to assimilate into humanoid society.

  • Garak, tailor who endlessly obfuscates his past and has decided to remain on this station for mysterious reasons.

  • Dukat, a Cardassian officer who previously resided over the occupation of Bajor on Deep Space Nine.

  • DS9 Crew with Jake Sisko, Chief O'Brien and Dr Bashir (left to right).

  • Worf, transfers to Deep Space Nine from the previous Star Trek show, Strategic Operations Officer.

Deep Space Nine touches on many facets of its universe, often drawing from our past and modern complexes.
